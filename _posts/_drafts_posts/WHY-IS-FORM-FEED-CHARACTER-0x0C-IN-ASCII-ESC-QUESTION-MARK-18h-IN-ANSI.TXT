
The ASCII Form Feed character (FF), represented as 0x0C or \f, and the ANSI escape sequence ESC[?18h serve different purposes and are not directly equivalent.

Form Feed (0x0C):
This is a control character in the ASCII standard.
Its primary historical function was to instruct a printer to eject the current page and advance to the top of the next page.
In modern contexts, it can be used as a page break in text editors or as a logical section separator.

ANSI Escape Sequence ESC[?18h:
This is an ANSI escape code used to control specific features of a terminal or display device.
The sequence ESC[?18h specifically enables "Printer bypass mode" in some terminals.
This mode allows data to be sent directly to a printer connected to the terminal, bypassing the terminal's display.
It is not a direct representation of a page break but rather a command to manage the terminal's interaction with an attached printer. 

Therefore, while both relate to printing or display control, their functions and interpretations are distinct.
The Form Feed character directly signals a page break, while the ANSI escape sequence controls a specific operational mode of the terminal regarding printer interaction.
